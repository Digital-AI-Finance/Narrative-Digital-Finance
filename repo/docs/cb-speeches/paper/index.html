Title

Central Bank Communication and Macroeconomic Conditions: A PCA-Based Framework for Analyzing Narrative-Reality Disconnect

Authors

Joerg Osterrieder, [Additional Authors]

Bern University of Applied Sciences, University of Twente

Abstract

This paper investigates the relationship between Federal Reserve speech sentiment and macroeconomic conditions using a comprehensive framework combining Principal Component Analysis (PCA), structural break detection (PELT algorithm), and rolling regression analysis. Analyzing 2,421 US Fed speeches from 1996-2025 alongside six FRED macroeconomic indicators, we construct orthogonal indices capturing macro strength and inflation dynamics. Our key finding reveals a near-zero correlation (0.005) between central bank speech sentiment and macroeconomic indices, challenging conventional assumptions about the effectiveness of narrative-driven policy transmission. The strong negative autocorrelation (-0.43) in hawkish sentiment suggests rapid mean reversion in central bank communication, while PELT-detected breakpoints identify distinct macroeconomic regimes. These results have important implications for understanding central bank communication effectiveness and the disconnect between economic narratives and underlying conditions.

Target Journal

Journal of Monetary Economics / Journal of Financial Economics
