1.1 Achievement of Research Objectives (mandatory)
To which extent have you achieved the research objectives? Describe the major accomplishments and outcomes of the project in detail, directly relating them to the objectives stated in your research plan. (~0.5-1.5 pages, up to 6000 characters)

The project successfully achieved all research objectives across the four work packages defined in the research plan, producing significant methodological and empirical contributions to the field of narrative digital finance.

WP1: Text Data & Text Analytics

We developed comprehensive NLP frameworks for financial text analysis. The TOPol (Topic-Orientation Polarity) framework provides a semi-supervised approach for reconstructing multidimensional semantic polarity fields using transformer embeddings, UMAP projection, and Leiden clustering. This moves beyond traditional unidimensional sentiment scoring to capture the full complexity of financial narratives. Additionally, we created an AI-enhanced systematic literature review methodology using NLP techniques, clustering algorithms, and interpretability tools, currently under revision at Financial Innovation journal.

WP2: Structural Breaks Detection & Asset Price Bubbles

We developed methodologies for detecting structural breaks in financial markets. The macro narratives framework combines a PCA-based US Macro Strength Index (using FED Funds Rate, CPI, PPI, GDP, Unemployment, Nonfarm Payrolls from St. Louis FED FRED) with the PELT algorithm for change point detection. Applied to Federal Reserve communications around the 2008 financial crisis, this approach successfully identified narrative shifts coinciding with macroeconomic regime changes.

WP3: Narratives for Structural Breaks

We established foundational frameworks for understanding how narratives shape financial markets. The "Primer on Narrative Finance" provides theoretical grounding, while the "Multimodal Influence" paper extends traditional NLP to include non-textual data (images, video, audio) for financial instrument pricing using Generative AI. The BERTopic-based narrative shift analysis quantifies polarity drift, semantic centroid movement, and keyword evolution across economic regimes.

WP4: Multidimensional AI and ML Solutions

In collaboration with Deutsche Borse, we developed novel methodology for classifying market participants based on nanosecond-level reaction times. The HFT Market Microstructure framework distinguishes Ultra-Fast Traders (UFT, <1 microsecond), High-Frequency Traders (HFT, 1-10 microseconds), and conventional participants using Xetra and Eurex trading data. This research documents participation shares, price discovery via mark-out signal-to-noise ratio, and market quality metrics for Euro STOXX 50 Index Futures.

1.2 Challenges, Negative Results and Unexpected Outcomes
Discuss challenges encountered, significant modifications to research plan, and negative or unexpected results. (up to 0.5 pages, max 2000 characters)

Data Access Challenges

Accessing high-quality financial text data required developing custom pipelines for multiple sources: RavenPack (news headlines), LSEG/Refinitiv (earnings call transcripts), BIS Gigando (central bank speeches), and SEC EDGAR (regulatory filings). The heterogeneity of text formats and temporal coverage required significant preprocessing effort.

Methodological Evolution

The original focus on unidimensional sentiment analysis evolved toward multidimensional semantic polarity (TOPol framework) after recognizing that financial narratives cannot be reduced to simple positive/negative scales. This adaptation strengthened the methodological contribution.

Unexpected Opportunity: Deutsche Borse Collaboration

Access to nanosecond-level trading data from Deutsche Borse exceeded initial expectations, enabling the HFT market microstructure research that was not originally planned but proved highly complementary to the narrative analysis objectives.

Project Overview

The proposed work program comprises four work packages (WP) with a conceptually new approach compared to existing research in financial market analysis:

  • Limited studies employing narratives and textual information in financial market analysis
  • Unexplored emotional evolution - studies on bubbles have largely ignored narratives underlying times of change
  • Methodological gap - finance research has not kept pace with advances in automated text analysis and large text corpora

Our empirical setup studies the interplay of narratives, language evolution, financial innovation, and market performance in a comprehensive cross-layer framework where evolutions, causality, and interactions can be measured explicitly.

Main Research Questions (from Application)

In what sense are financial markets (ex-ante) predictable?

Is the ex-ante forecastability persistent, can it be applied for real use cases and to which extent?

How can structural break detection and changes in financial time series improve and complement modern portfolio theory?

Original Work Packages

WP1: Text Data & Text Analytics

NLP and text mining techniques for asset allocation and prediction, combined with structural breaks and change point detection methodology. Focus on classic financial assets which tend to be under-researched.

RQs: Efficient use of textual analysis for portfolio management; Most promising NLP techniques

WP2: Structural Breaks Detection & Asset Price Bubbles

Three-step approach: (1) Post-ante detection in historical data; (2) Live detection validation; (3) NLP as supporting method for detecting breakpoints.

RQs: Detect/identify/date structural breaks; Live detection using alternative data

WP3: Narratives for Structural Breaks

Narratives "go viral" with economic impact (Shiller 2017). Use pre-trained Transformers (BERT), text matching with low-dimensional embeddings, 2x2 experimental designs.

RQs: Can narratives predict/detect bubbles; Can narratives sway investment opinions

WP4: Multidimensional AI and ML Solutions

Combine methods from WP1-3 into a fully integrated framework. Check if combined ML approach outperforms single methods.

RQs: Combined ML vs single methods; Complex AI vs simple forecast combinations

Expected Outcomes (from Application)

Unique Contributions: (1) Exclusive focus on ex-ante forecasting (live detection), easily adjustable to rapidly changing markets; (2) Combining existing data sources, developing new quantitative models, and new frameworks to understand markets.

Enhanced Prediction Models

Financial market models with greater accuracy in detecting asset price bubbles and structural breaks

Novel Datasets & Methods

Deeper insights into the interplay between market narratives and financial indicators

Practical Tools

For asset management and regulatory bodies to better anticipate and react to market crises
