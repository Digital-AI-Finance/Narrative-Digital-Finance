Executive Summary

This project successfully developed novel computational frameworks bridging narrative economics with financial market analysis. Key achievements include: (1) the TOPol framework for multidimensional semantic polarity analysis, (2) macro-narrative integration combining macroeconomic indices with topic modeling for structural break detection, and (3) HFT classification methodology measuring how rapidly narratives propagate through markets at nanosecond resolution. The research produced 6 publications, established collaborations with Deutsche Borse AG and Quoniam Asset Management, and created open-source tools (OSF: osf.io/nr94j) enabling reproducible research. The project extends narrative economics (Shiller 2017, 2019) with quantitative computational methods.

1.1 Achievement of Research Objectives

The project addressed three central research questions from the original proposal: (1) In what sense are financial markets ex-ante predictable through narrative analysis? (2) Is ex-ante forecastability persistent and applicable for real use cases? (3) How can structural break detection using narratives improve modern portfolio theory? We achieved substantial progress across all four work packages.

Completed

WP1: Text Data & Text Analytics

Developed the TOPol framework: semi-unsupervised multidimensional semantic polarity using transformer embeddings, UMAP projection, and Leiden clustering. Moves beyond unidimensional sentiment to capture narrative complexity. Code released: osf.io/nr94j. AI-enhanced SLR methodology under revision at Financial Innovation.

Completed

WP2: Structural Breaks & Bubbles

Macro Narratives Framework: PCA-based US Macro Strength Index (FRED data 1996-2025) combined with PELT algorithm. Applied to Fed speeches (BIS Gigando 2004-2010) around 2008 crisis. BERTopic for narrative shifts. Statistical validation via 1,000 random shuffling simulations.

Completed

WP3: Narratives for Structural Breaks

Multimodal Influence Framework (SSRN 4698153): Extends NLP to images, video, audio for financial pricing. BERTopic-based analysis quantifies polarity drift (FinBERT), semantic centroid movement (cosine similarity), and keyword evolution (Jaccard MMR) across economic regimes.

Completed

WP4: Multidimensional AI/ML

HFT Market Microstructure with Deutsche Borse: Measures narrative propagation speed through markets at nanosecond resolution. Classifies UFT (<1 microsecond), HFT (1-10 microseconds), conventional (>10 microseconds). Documents 20% UFT/HFT participation share.

1.2 Methodological Evolution & Outcomes

Data Infrastructure Evolution

The original proposal envisioned Bloomberg and Refinitiv terminals as primary data sources. We developed custom data pipelines for alternative sources that proved more suitable: RavenPack (financial news headlines), LSEG (earnings call transcripts), BIS Gigando (worldwide central bank speeches), SEC EDGAR (regulatory filings), and Deutsche Borse (nanosecond-level trading data). These alternatives provide both rich textual narratives and precise market reaction timestamps unavailable through terminal-based access.

Computational Approach Advantages

The original WP3.1 design planned controlled 2x2 investment experiments. The methodology evolved toward the Multimodal Influence Framework (SSRN 4698153), providing computational analysis at unprecedented scale. This evolution was driven by: (1) scalability requirements for analyzing exponentially growing data volumes (dozens of exabytes in 2003 to 180 zettabytes by 2025), (2) reproducibility advantages of computational methods, and (3) opportunity to extend beyond text to multimodal analysis including images, video, and audio. The TOPol framework similarly moved beyond unidimensional sentiment scoring to capture multidimensional semantic polarity fields.

HFT-Narrative Integration

The Deutsche Borse collaboration (initiated January 2024) enabled a novel research stream: measuring how rapidly narratives propagate through financial markets. Access to proprietary nanosecond-level trading data from the T7 platform allowed classification of market participants by their reaction speed to information: Ultra-Fast Traders (UFT, <1 microsecond using FPGAs), High-Frequency Traders (HFT, 1-10 microseconds), and conventional participants (>10 microseconds). This directly addresses the original research question of ex-ante predictability by quantifying how quickly narrative-driven information is incorporated into prices.

Future Research Directions

The computational frameworks developed enable several extensions: (1) Real-time narrative monitoring systems for live structural break detection, building on the post-ante validation demonstrated with the 2008 crisis case study; (2) Cross-market narrative propagation analysis using the HFT classification methodology; (3) Integration of the TOPol polarity fields with volatility regime detection through the PhD research (Stages 3-5, 2026-2027).

1.3 Contribution to Knowledge Advancement

This research bridges narrative economics (Shiller, 2017, 2019) with computational linguistics and financial econometrics, providing quantitative tools for analyzing how narratives influence market dynamics. The work addresses a fundamental question: how do narrative forms influence volatility and regime changes in financial markets?

TOPol Framework

First semi-unsupervised framework for multidimensional semantic polarity in financial texts. Unlike traditional unidimensional sentiment scoring (positive/negative), TOPol captures narrative complexity through: transformer embeddings for semantic representation, UMAP projection for dimensionality reduction, and Leiden clustering for topic segmentation. The vectorial representation enables quantification of magnitude, direction, and semantic meaning of polarity shifts across discourse regimes. Open source release at osf.io/nr94j enables replication and extension by the research community.

Macro-Narrative Integration

Novel methodology combining PCA-based macroeconomic indices (US Macro Strength Index from FRED data: FED Funds Rate, CPI, PPI, GDP, Unemployment, Nonfarm Payrolls) with BERTopic topic modeling for detecting narrative regime shifts at structural breakpoints. Null hypothesis testing via 1,000 random shuffling simulations provides rigorous statistical validation. The framework quantifies polarity drift (FinBERT sentiment), semantic centroid movement (cosine similarity), and keyword evolution (Jaccard similarity of MMR sets).

HFT Classification & Narrative Propagation

New latency-based taxonomy measuring narrative propagation speed through markets: UFT (<1 microsecond, FPGA-based), HFT (1-10 microseconds), and conventional participants (>10 microseconds). Validated on Deutsche Borse T7 platform data with documented participation shares (~20% UFT/HFT), price discovery metrics (15-second mark-out signal-to-noise ratio), and market quality indicators (order-imbalance volatility, Amihud illiquidity). This methodology enables regulatory monitoring and market surveillance applications.
